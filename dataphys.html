1. DATA PHYSICALIZATION - THE SOUND OF DEATH

Info Are.na E-Mail
Menu




— This project is a data physicalization project wherein we worked to physically represent real-life, credible data sets. The data I chose to represent is the Deaths by School Shootings in the US from 2009-2018 and the link to the Data Source can be found here: https://www.cnn.com/interactive/2019/07/us/ten-years-of-school-shootings-trnd/. I created a windchime to induce the senses of sight and sound as an attempt to communicate the data I chose.






—Fabrication techniques:
1. Drilling (wood and metal)
2. Jewellery saw (metal)
3. Sanding (wood)
4. Staining (wood)

—Materials used:
1. Polycotton Thread
2. Copper tubes (hollow)
3. Wood panel (found object)
4. Black stain (wood)




I wanted to create a lot of intentionality behind the materials used in constructing this windchime to create separations from commercial windchimes that can be found in stores like Homegoods etc. It was also extremely important for me to have most of it handmade because the topic itself is very personal to me.

I used copper tubes because bullets typically have a hard copper jacket to encase the lead and I wanted to reference the bullets somehow. I used the wooded panel that I found in the wood studio and sanded it down, drilled holes in it and stained it black because it is a colour of grief and death and seemed appropriate. I used the polycotton thread to represent the fragility of life and allow the copper logs to stand out.

I wanted to create a sense that something that isn’t completely perfect can create such a delicate sound and with the context of the data itself, force one to think. Also, being an international student I also had a budget restriction and was happy with what I was able to achieve myself physically because the sanding, staining, drilling and tying was very labour-intensive and skills that I had to acquire along the way.
